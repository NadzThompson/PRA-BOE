# Composites

1

###  Application

1.1

Unless otherwise stated, this Part applies to:

  1. (1) a _UK Solvency II firm_ ;
  2. (2) in accordance with [Insurance General Application 3](/pra-rules/insurance-general-application#2e5a9cd32e6d4026895a5d543b990108 "Insurance General Application 3"), the _Society_; and
  3. (3) in accordance with [Insurance General Application 3](/pra-rules/insurance-general-application#2e5a9cd32e6d4026895a5d543b990108 "Insurance General Application 3"), _managing agents_.

*Effective: 01/01/2016*

2

###  Long-Term and General Insurance Activities to Be Separately Managed

2.1

This Chapter:

  1. (1) applies to a _composite firm_ other than a _pure reinsurer_ ; and
  2. (2) does not apply to a _managing agent_ which manages one or more _syndicates_ , all of which carry on _reinsurance_ exclusively.

*Effective: 01/01/2016*

2.2

A _composite firm_ must separately manage the activities relating to its _general insurance business_ and the activities relating to its _long-term insurance business_ in such a way that:

  1. (1) its _long-term insurance business_ and its _general insurance business_ are distinct from one another;
  2. (2) the interests of _policyholders_ of _contracts of long-term insurance_ are not prejudiced by activities relating to the _firm’s_ _general insurance business_ and the interests of _policyholders_ of _contracts of general insurance_ are not prejudiced by activities relating to the _firm’s_ _long-term insurance business_ ; and
  3. (3) profits from the activities relating to the _composite firm’s_ _long-term insurance business_ benefit _policyholders_ of _contracts of long-term insurance_ as if the _composite firm_ was engaged only in _long-term insurance business_.

**[Note: Art. 74(1) of the _Solvency II Directive_]**

*Effective: 01/01/2016*

3

###  Minimum Financial Obligations: General Provisions

3.1

This Chapter:

  1. (1) applies to a _composite firm_ , other than a _pure reinsurer_ ; and
  2. (2) does not apply to a _managing agent_ which manages one or more _syndicates_ , all of which carry on _reinsurance_ exclusively.

*Effective: 01/01/2016*

3.2

A _composite firm_ must maintain separate accounts for each of its _long-term insurance business_ and its _general insurance business_ to show the sources of the results for each activity separately.

*Effective: 01/01/2016*

3.3

For the purposes of [3.2](/pra-rules/composites#46ee4210d93e401bbd73cdafebe4fa98 "3.2"), the _firm_ must:

  1. (1) break down, according to origin, all income (including _premiums_ , recoverables from _reinsurance contracts_ and investment income) and all expenditure (including insurance settlements, additions to _technical provisions_ , _reinsurance premiums_ and operating expenses) in respect of its _general insurance business_ and its _long-term insurance business_ , respectively; and
  2. (2) if items are shared between the _firm’s_ _long-term insurance business_ and its _general insurance business_ , apportion those items appropriately between the two activities and enter them into the accounts on the basis of that apportionment.

**[Note: Art. 74(6) of the _Solvency II Directive_]**

*Effective: 01/01/2016*

3.4

The _firm_ must record the methods on the basis of which the apportionment referred to in [3.3](/pra-rules/composites#11b20d1c830146b69a8329d276966b2a "3.3")(2) has been made and be able to demonstrate to the _PRA_ the appropriateness of those methods of apportionment.

*Effective: 01/01/2016*

4

###  Minimum Financial Obligations: Calculation of Notional Minimum Capital Requirement

4.1

This Chapter:

  1. (1) applies to a _composite firm_ , other than a _pure reinsurer_ ; and
  2. (2) does not apply to _managing agents_.

*Effective: 01/01/2016*

4.2

Without prejudice to the _SCR Rules_ and the [Minimum Capital Requirement Part](/pra-rules/minimum-capital-requirement), the _firm_ must calculate on the basis of the accounts referred to in [3.2](/pra-rules/composites#46ee4210d93e401bbd73cdafebe4fa98 "3.2"):

  1. (1) a notional long-term insurance MCR with respect to its _long-term insurance business_ , calculated as if the _firm_ carried on _long-term insurance business_ only; and
  2. (2) a notional general insurance MCR with respect to its _general insurance business_ , calculated as if the _firm_ carried on _general insurance business_ only.

**[Note: Art. 74(2) of the _Solvency II Directive_]**

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4.3

The _firm_ must cover:

  1. (1) its notional long-term insurance MCR with _eligible own funds_ attributable to its _long-term insurance business_ , as identified on the basis of the accounts referred to in [3.2](/pra-rules/composites#46ee4210d93e401bbd73cdafebe4fa98 "3.2"); and
  2. (2) its notional general insurance MCR with _eligible own funds_ attributable to its _general insurance business_ , as identified on the basis of the accounts referred to in [3.2](/pra-rules/composites#46ee4210d93e401bbd73cdafebe4fa98 "3.2").

**[Note: Art. 74(3) of theSolvency II Directive]**

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4.4

For the purposes of [4.3](/pra-rules/composites#d4572dada17e484a8164fc191d7b6b12 "4.3"), the _firm_ must not cover:

  1. (1) its notional long-term insurance MCR with _eligible own funds_ attributable to its _general insurance business_ ; and
  2. (2) its notional general insurance MCR with _eligible own funds_ attributable to its _long-term insurance business_.

**[Note: Art. 74(3) of the _Solvency II Directive_]**

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4.5

The _firm_ must prepare a statement on the basis of the accounts referred to in [3.2](/pra-rules/composites#46ee4210d93e401bbd73cdafebe4fa98 "3.2") identifying the _eligible own funds_ covering the notional long-term insurance MCR and the notional general insurance MCR, respectively.

**[Note: Art. 74(6) of the _Solvency II Directive_]**

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4.6

Provided the _firm_ satisfies the requirements in [4.3](/pra-rules/composites#d4572dada17e484a8164fc191d7b6b12 "4.3") and [4.4](/pra-rules/composites#88082a04108547a588e98c32c06e6425 "4.4"), and subject to the requirement in [4.7](/pra-rules/composites#e6c29436d3ee4dc4aa17df2f4a65d7c2 "4.7"), a _firm_ may use:

  1. (1) _eligible own funds_ attributable to its _general insurance business_ that are in excess of its notional general insurance MCR; and
  2. (2) _eligible own funds_ attributable to its _long-term insurance business_ that are in excess of its notional long-term insurance MCR;

to cover part or all of the difference between the _firm’s SCR_ and the sum of its notional general insurance MCR and notional long-term insurance MCR.

**[Note: Art. 74(4) of the _Solvency II Directive_]**

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4.7

For the purposes of [4.6](/pra-rules/composites#6bdc4807ebc541e0b67987d9516650bf "4.6"), a _firm_ must notify the _PRA_ before using:

  1. (1) _eligible own funds_ referred to in [4.6](/pra-rules/composites#6bdc4807ebc541e0b67987d9516650bf "4.6")(1) to cover the portion of the difference referred to in [4.6](/pra-rules/composites#6bdc4807ebc541e0b67987d9516650bf "4.6") that relates to the difference between the notional long-term insurance SCR and the notional long-term insurance MCR; or
  2. (2) _eligible own funds_ referred to in [4.6](/pra-rules/composites#6bdc4807ebc541e0b67987d9516650bf "4.6")(2) to cover the portion of the difference referred to in [4.6](/pra-rules/composites#6bdc4807ebc541e0b67987d9516650bf "4.6") that relates to the difference between the notional general insurance SCR and the notional general insurance MCR.

**[Note: Art. 74(4) of the _Solvency II Directive_]**

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4.8

If a _composite firm_ is in breach of either [4.3](/pra-rules/composites#d4572dada17e484a8164fc191d7b6b12 "4.3")(1) or [4.3](/pra-rules/composites#d4572dada17e484a8164fc191d7b6b12 "4.3")(2), [Undertakings In Difficulty 4.1](/pra-rules/undertakings-in-difficulty#6bfda81291f249ff8be66752faf30290 "Undertakings In Difficulty 4.1") applies to the activity in respect of which the breach has occurred, as if the words ‘ _MCR_ ’ in [Undertakings In Difficulty 4.1](/pra-rules/undertakings-in-difficulty#6bfda81291f249ff8be66752faf30290 "Undertakings In Difficulty 4.1") were substituted with the words ‘notional long-term insurance MCR’ or ‘notional general insurance MCR’, as applicable, regardless of whether any breach has occurred in respect of the other activity.

**[Note: Art. 74(7) of the _Solvency II Directive_]**

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4A

###  Notional General Insurance Minimum Capital Requirement

4A.1

This Chapter:

  1. (1) applies to a composite firm, other than a pure reinsurer; and
  2. (2) does not apply to managing agents.

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4A.2

The notional general insurance MCR must be equal to the following:

\\[\mathrm{NMCR_{gi}\ =\ max(NMCR_{(combined,gi)},\ AMCR_{gi})}\\]

where:

  1. (1) NMCR(combined,gi) denotes the notional combined general insurance minimum capital requirement prescribed in [4A.3](/pra-rules/composites#a3d782cd0b904ae29c86c7978000d574); and
  2. (2) AMCRgi denotes:
     1. (a) the absolute floor prescribed in [Minimum Capital Requirement 3.2(1)](/pra-rules/minimum-capital-requirement#965c1f3c028f41a3a7128379f39abac9); or
     2. (b) where [Minimum Capital Requirement 3.2(5)](/pra-rules/minimum-capital-requirement#965c1f3c028f41a3a7128379f39abac9) applies, the sum of the absolute floor prescribed in [Minimum Capital Requirement 3.2(2)](/pra-rules/minimum-capital-requirement#965c1f3c028f41a3a7128379f39abac9).

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4A.3

The notional combined general insurance minimum capital requirement must be equal to the following:

\\[\mathrm{NMCR_{(combined,gi)}\ =\ min(max(NMCR_{(linear,gi)},\ 0.25 \cdot \ (NSCR_{gi}\ +\ Addon_{gi})),\ 0.45 \cdot \ (NSCR_{gi}\ +\ Addon_{gi}))}\\]

where:

  1. (1) NMCR(linear,gi) denotes the notional linear minimum capital requirement for general insurance business prescribed in [4A.4](/pra-rules/composites#4bdb91d1cee947e3822437e7a62db9a5);
  2. (2) NSCRgi denotes the notional general insurance SCR; and
  3. (3) Addongi denotes the part of any capital add-on imposed by the PRA, which has been apportioned by PRA to the general insurance business of the firm.

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4A.4

The notional linear minimum capital requirement for general insurance business must be equal to the following:

\\[\mathrm{NMCR_{(linear,gi)}\ =\ MCR_{(gi,gi)}\ +\ MCR_{(l,gi)}}\\]

where:

  1. (1) MCR(gi,gi) denotes the linear formula component for general insurance and reinsurance obligations relating to general insurance business calculated in accordance with [4A.5](/pra-rules/composites#eee1662e335643d39943ed9aab7aa2e0); and
  2. (2)  MCR(l,gi) denotes the linear formula component for long-term insurance and reinsurance obligations relating to general insurance business calculated in accordance with [4A.5](/pra-rules/composites#eee1662e335643d39943ed9aab7aa2e0).

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4A.5

MCR(gi,gi) and MCR(l,gi) must be calculated in the same way as MCR(linear,gi) and MCR(linear,l) referred to in [Minimum Capital Requirement 3B](/pra-rules/minimum-capital-requirement#ff620ac3500f46a7b99366f57e0ddbc1) and [3C](/pra-rules/minimum-capital-requirement#38f0a95f0ca74a108e6394f7685d25e9) respectively, but the technical provisions or premiums written used in the calculation must only relate to the firm’s general insurance and reinsurance obligations.

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4B

###  Notional Long-term Minimum Capital Requirement

4B.1

This Chapter:

  1. (1) applies to a composite firm, other than a pure reinsurer; and
  2. (2) does not apply to managing agents.

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4B.2

The notional long-term insurance MCR must be equal to the following:

\\[\mathrm{NMCR_{l}\ =\ max(NMCR_{(combined,l)},\ AMCR_{l})}\\]

where:

  1. (1) NMCR(combined,l) denotes the notional combined long-term insurance minimum capital requirement prescribed in [4B.3](/pra-rules/composites#d62fd60354154c9b878cc278f85b2e12); and
  2. (2) AMCRl denotes the absolute floor prescribed in [Minimum Capital Requirement 3.2(2)](/pra-rules/minimum-capital-requirement#965c1f3c028f41a3a7128379f39abac9).

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4B.3

The notional combined long-term insurance minimum capital requirement must be equal to the following:

\\[\mathrm{NMCR_{(combined,l)}\ =\ min(max(NMCR_{(linear,l)},\ 0.25\ \cdot \ (NSCR_{I}\ +\ Addon_{l})),\ 0.45\ \cdot \ (NSCR_{I}\ +\ Addon_{l})}\\]

where:

  1. (1) NMCR(linear,l) denotes the notional linear minimum capital requirement for long-term insurance business prescribed in 4B.4;
  2. (2) NSCRl denotes the notional long-term insurance SCR; and
  3. (3) Addonl denotes the part of any capital add-on imposed by the PRA, which has been apportioned by PRA to the long-term insurance business of the firm.

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4B.4

The notional linear minimum capital requirement for long-term insurance business must be equal to the following:

\\[\mathrm{NMCR_{(linear,l)}\ =\ MCR_{(gi,l)}\ +\ MCR_{(l,l)}}\\]

where:

  1. (1) MCR(gi,l) denotes the linear formula component for general insurance and reinsurance obligations relating to long-term insurance business calculated in accordance with [4B.5](/pra-rules/composites#3aacdf6dea0a4ee689eb631e7532f9f4); and
  2. (2) MCR(l,l) denotes the linear formula component for long-term insurance and reinsurance obligations relating to long-term insurance business calculated in accordance with [4B.5](/pra-rules/composites#3aacdf6dea0a4ee689eb631e7532f9f4).

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4B.5

MCR(gi,l) and MCR(l,l) must be calculated in the same way as MCR(linear,gi) and MCR(linear,l) referred to in [Minimum Capital Requirement 3B](/pra-rules/minimum-capital-requirement#ff620ac3500f46a7b99366f57e0ddbc1) and [3C](/pra-rules/minimum-capital-requirement#38f0a95f0ca74a108e6394f7685d25e9) respectively, but the technical provisions or premiums written used in the calculation must only relate to long-term insurance and reinsurance obligations.

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4C

###  Notional Solvency Capital Requirement

4C.1

This Chapter:

  1. (1) applies to a composite firm, other than a pure reinsurer; and
  2. (2) does not apply to managing agents.

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4C.2

The firm must calculate a notional general insurance SCR and a notional long-term insurance SCR.

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

4C.3

The notional general insurance SCR must be equal to the following:

\\[{\mathrm{NSCR}}_{\mathrm{gi}}\mathrm{\ =\ }\frac{{\mathrm{NMCR}}_{\mathrm{(linear,gi)}}}{{\mathrm{NMCR}}_{\left(\mathrm{linear,gi}\right)\mathrm{\ } }\mathrm{+\ } {\mathrm{NMCR}}_{\mathrm{(linear,l)}}}\mathrm{\cdot \ SCR}\\]

where:

  1. (1) SCR denotes the firm’s SCR, which must for the purposes of this Chapter exclude any capital add-on;
  2. (2) NMCR(linear,gi) denotes the notional linear general insurance minimum capital requirement for general insurance business prescribed in [4A.4](/pra-rules/composites#4bdb91d1cee947e3822437e7a62db9a5); and
  3. (3) NMCR(linear,l) denotes the notional linear minimum capital requirement for long-term insurance business prescribed in [4B.4](/pra-rules/composites#48571a634e94406cb7d7b65d9856e48c).

*Effective: 24/07/2025*

4C.4

The notional long-term insurance SCR must be equal to the following:

\\[{\mathrm{NSCR}}_{\mathrm{l}}\mathrm{\ =\ }\frac{{\mathrm{NMCR}}_{\mathrm{(linear,l)}}}{{\mathrm{NMCR}}_{\left(\mathrm{linear,gi}\right)\mathrm{\ } }\mathrm{+\ } {\mathrm{NMCR}}_{\mathrm{(linear,l)}}}\mathrm{\cdot \ SCR}\\]

where:

  1. (1) SCR denotes the SCR, excluding any capital add-on;
  2. (2) NMCR(linear,gi) denotes the notional linear general insurance minimum capital requirement for general insurance business prescribed in [4A.4](/pra-rules/composites#4bdb91d1cee947e3822437e7a62db9a5); and
  3. (3) NMCR(linear,l) denotes the notional linear minimum capital requirement for long-term insurance business prescribed in [4B.4](/pra-rules/composites#48571a634e94406cb7d7b65d9856e48c).

*Effective: 31/12/2024*

5

###  Links Between General Insurers and Long-Term Insurers

5.1

If a _general insurer_ and a _long-term insurer_ have financial, commercial or administrative links with each other, each of those _firms_ must ensure that its accounts are not distorted by an agreement between them or by any arrangement which could affect the apportionment of expenses and income.

**[Note: Art. 73(4) of the _Solvency II Directive_]**

*Effective: 01/01/2016*

6

###  Lloyd’s

6.1

This Chapter applies to _managing agents_.

*Effective: 01/01/2016*

6.2

A _managing agent_ must not permit both _general insurance business_ and _long-term insurance business_ to be carried on together through any _syndicate_ managed by it, except where:

  1. (1) the _long-term insurance business_ to be carried on by that _syndicate_ is or is to be restricted to _reinsurance_ ; or
  2. (2) the _general insurance business_ to be carried on by that _syndicate_ is or is to be restricted to _effect contracts of insurance_ or _carry out contracts of insurance_ in _general insurance business_ class 1 (accident) or class 2 (sickness).

**[Note: Art. 73(2) of the _Solvency II Directive_]**

*Effective: 31/12/2024*
